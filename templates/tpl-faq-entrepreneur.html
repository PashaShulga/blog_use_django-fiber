Entrepreneur Questions

How do I apply to have my company listed on GrowthFountain?

The process of listing on GrowthFountain begins when you register as an Entrepreneur on the GrowthFountain website. The registration process includes a series of questions that intended to help you in defining your company and its particular needs.

Can non-U.S. businesses list on GrowthFountain?

No. At this time, the GrowthFountain website is available only to companies based in the United States or any territory thereof.

How much does GrowthFountain cost?

For companies that successfully raise money on GrowthFountain, we charge 5% of the money raised. If you don't reach your goal, you will not owe us any money. There are some minimal upfront costs that we'll pass along to issuers prior to listing - including the cost of running background checks on officers, directors and 20%+ voting shareholders and the third party escrow agent fee. And we'll pass along a $10 payment processing fee to investors.

Is my business information confidential?

GrowthFountain offers companies the ability to grant or deny access to any investor wishing to view specific information about the company’s business and offering. However, you should consider some level of disclosure about your business plan to be a necessary component of raising funds.

Can GrowthFountain help with legal docs for my upcoming transaction?

As a way of assisting both our listing companies and our investors, GrowthFountain makes template documents available for use in connection with financings. GrowthFountain, however, is not a legal advisor and does not purport to provide legal advice. We recommend in all cases that people discuss the offerings and investments with their own legal counsel.

Do I need to negotiate separately with each investor?

No. All offering terms will be determined in advance and all investors will receive the same terms.

How much ownership will I have to give to an investor?

The specific terms of each offering are ultimately decided between the entrepreneurs and the investors.

How long will a fundraising take?

The length of time required to complete a fundraise will vary from case to case. The amount of time required will depend on several factors, such the amount sought, the terms of the offering, the speed with which the company responds to information requests, and the degree to which the company may be otherwise represented in social media. All offerings must remain open for a minimum of 21 days. You should anticipate a minimum of 2-3 months for the completion of the offering and offerings may end up remaining open for significantly longer.

How do I communicate with investors after the fundraising?

Companies that issue securities through GrowthFountain will be required to obtain a registered transfer agent. Not to worry, we’ll guide you through the process and ensure an easy and natural workflow – and this transfer agent will enable you to easily manage investors, including sending emails, updating information, uploading supporting documents, making payments and integrating with secondary market platforms.
